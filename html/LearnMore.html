Enough Jargon:

AI Dictionary

Machine Learning (ML)
A way for computers to learn from data instead of following fixed instructions. Example: Netflix recommends movies based on what you watch.
Neural Networks
AI models inspired by the human brain, used for tasks like facial recognition.
Deep Learning
A more advanced type of machine learning with many-layered neural networks. Powers ChatGPT and self-driving cars.
Large Language Models (LLMs)
AI trained on huge amounts of text to generate human-like responses. Example: ChatGPT.
Generative AI
AI that creates new content like text, images, and music. Example: DALL·E makes AI-generated art.
Computer Vision
AI that helps computers see and understand images. Example: Face ID or self-driving car cameras.
Natural Language Processing (NLP)
AI that understands and processes human language. Example: Siri, Alexa, Google Translate.
Reinforcement Learning
AI that learns by trial and error, like a dog learning tricks with rewards.
Bias in AI
When AI makes unfair decisions because it learned from biased data. Example: AI hiring systems favoring certain resumes.
AI Hallucination
When AI confidently makes up false information. Example: ChatGPT inventing fake research papers.
Singularity
A hypothetical future where AI surpasses human intelligence. Often seen in sci-fi movies.
AGI (Artificial General Intelligence)
An AI as smart as a human in all tasks, not just one specialized job.
